THERE ARE SO MANY STRATEGIES OR APPROACHES TO MITIGATE THE URBAN HEAT ISLAND EFFECT BY DOING SOME THINGS WITH ARCHITECTURE AND COMBINING NATURE FOR A SUSTAINABLE URBAN DESIGN.

GREEN ROOFTOP

These are roofs covered with vegetation, offering benefits like insulation, stormwater management, and urban biodiversity, plus it reduces the urban heat island effect."

WHITE SURFACE

"white surface are efficient with reflecting the sun rays. This reflectivity, known as albedo, helps reduce heat absorption, leading to lower energy consumption for cooling and improved thermal comfort."

URBAN BIOSWALES

"Urban bioswales are vegetated landscape features designed to manage stormwater runoff and improve water quality in urban areas by capturing, filtering, and infiltrating rainwater."

URBAN FOREST

"These are collections of trees and other vegetation in urban areas, providing shade, cooling, air purification, and habitat for wildlife."

URBAN GREEN WALLS

"These are plant-covered walls, adding greenery to urban spaces and offering air purification and aesthetic appeal."

URBAN GREEN CORRIDORS

"Urban green corridors are linear green spaces, such as greenways or tree-lined streets that reduced air and noise pollution, and a cooler urban environment by helping prevent heat islands"

URBAN PONDS

"Urban ponds are small, natural or man-made bodies of water within cities, often serving as stormwater management, biodiversity havens, and recreational spaces."

URBAN WETLANDS

"Cities designed to absorb and manage rainwater, reducing flooding and improving water quality."

BIOPHILIC DESIGN

"This approach integrates nature into building and public spaces, enhancing well-being and connecting people with nature."
